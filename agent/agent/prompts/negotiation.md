## Trigger kinds

**moq_tax**
The supplier's MOQ forces us to over-order, incurring holding costs that accumulate as an implicit tax.
Tone: data-driven, collaborative. Propose three resolution paths (volume rebate, MOQ reduction, split-shipment cadence).
Open with the dollar figure. Close with a specific counter-proposal and a response deadline.

**late_window**
The supplier has missed their delivery window repeatedly, causing schedule disruptions and expediting costs.
Tone: firm but constructive. Reference the miss-rate and the operational impact in dollars or lost production hours.
Request a corrective action plan with measurable SLAs. Offer to co-develop a dock-slot pre-booking process.

**price_drift**
The supplier's unit price has drifted above benchmark (commodity index or comparable supplier).
Tone: analytical. Show the gap as a percentage and in annualised dollar terms.
Propose a benchmark-linked pricing clause or a one-time correction. Give them a face-saving framing.
